HEBRON TSA


A Brief History

Hebron’s TSA Chapter was established in 1999, the same year the high school opened. With just 8 members at its launch, the team has grown tremendously in size as it presently has over 100 members, only looking to expand its reach and influence throughout the school. According to Scot Finch, the principal of Hebron High School, the reason for bringing TSA to Hebron at its opening was the need for an extracurricular club that encouraged the development of STEM-based classes, promoted leadership development, and included competitive opportunities for students. In the past, Engineering and Architectural Design instructor, Dale Rodgers, was the advisor and sponsor of Hebron TSA; however, he has recently left the school, leaving many of the responsibilities of running the chapter in the hands of the students. Hebron TSA hopes to continue to flourish this competitive season despite Mr. Rodger’s departure.

Instructors

Dale "Montana" Rodgers

A long time Architecture and Engineering Design teacher at Hebron High School, Dale Rodgers has been spearheading the Hebron TSA chapter for almost a decade. During his time, the club had drastically expanded and more students competed at the State and National levels with each progressing year. However, about a few weeks ago, Rodgers left his job at Hebron High School to work at the Kubota Corporation. Although Mr. Rodgers has left, he will be checking-up on and visiting the chapter frequently.

Dianne Weaver

Dianne Weaver has been teaching Fashion Design and co-leading the Hebron TSA Chapter for many years. After the departure of Dale Rodgers, Ms. Weaver was selected to lead the TSA Chapter at Hebron. She has graciously accepted the position, hoping to take the Hebron TSA to new heights and continue the legacy of Mr. Rodgers.

Welcome!

After a long competitive season, the Hebron TSA Webmaster team received 3rd place at the 2018 TSA National Conference. We'd like to thank our advisers, supporters, and everyone who was involved in the creation of this website. Feel free to look around!

President - Arpan Patel

Arpan has served in a myriad of leadership roles since joining TSA in 9th grade. His Biotechnology Design team has consistently placed high in the National TSA Conference. As a senior, Arpan hopes to study pre-med at UTD next year.

Vice President - Patrick Allen

A member of TSA since his freshman year, Patrick Allen has drastically improved the Hebron Robotics Team and is currently the team captain. As a senior, Patrick strives for his team to compete at the National TSA Conference. Patrick will be attending the University of Texas at Austin this upcoming year, studying Electrical Engineering.

Secretary - Rahul Venna

In his 4 years of being involved in TSA, Rahul has served various leadership positions and has consistently advanced to the State and National Conferences. This year, Rahul hopes to bring his Biotech team to Nationals once again. Rahul will be studying Biochemistry at the University of Texas at Dallas this upcoming year.

Treasurer - Ammar Dharani

A member of TSA since his sophomore year, Ammar has served vigorously to improve the TSA chapter by introducing interactive activities and increasing club membership. This year, Ammar is running for the Texas TSA State President and hopes to qualify for Nationals in the Webmaster event. Ammar hopes to study Biochemistry and Computer Science at Duke University.

Reporter - Riya Gupta

Riya has been involved in TSA since her freshman year and since then, she has qualified for Nationals in the SciVis event and has been a finalist for the Music Production at the State conference. After high school, Riya hopes to major in Bagpipe Studies at Carnegie Mellon University in Pittsburgh.

Sergeant of Arms - Alizer Khowaja

Alizer, a member of TSA since his freshman year, has played a key role in the Hebron chapter’s recovery after Mr. Rodgers had left. Throughout his 4 years in TSA, Alizer has consistently qualified and placed in the Region and State conference in both Technology Problem Solving and Dragster Design.
