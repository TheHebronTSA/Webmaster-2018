ZUBE Robotics

“ZUBE Robotics strives to improve the quality of life of consumers and make robots universally accessible”

Humble Beginnings

ZUBE Robotics was established when 3 high school friends had a seed of an idea that sprung into a tree. Since its inception, ZUBE Robotics has strived to introduce friendly companion robots to all spectrums of society from home to industry. The founders of ZUBE Robotics hope that each home, business and school will have a ZUBE robot and that the robots will increase the society’s standard of living.

The original ZUBE Concept created by Ammar as a young child.

A Global Reach

Although the company is headquartered in Carrollton, Texas, ZUBE Robotics serves people around the world. ZUBE Robotics consistently receives positive feedback from consumers of all walks of life including teachers, doctors, and first-responders. Because of this positive feedback, ZUBE Robotics is the global market leader for the development, manufacturing, and marketing of robots.

Introducing the ZUBE

The ZUBE Robot is a friendly, companion robot that can be used in all aspects of society. This revolutionary robot can protect your home, tutor your children, and clean your house. ZUBE is an open-source technology platform, allowing developers to study, change, and distribute the technology and apply it to new spheres such as medicine.

Uses of the ZUBE

Educational

"Edutainment"

  • Introduction to Programming
  • Storytelling and Online Lessons
  • Hide-And-Seek; Red Light, Green Light; and Other Interactive Games

Classroom Assistant

  • Increases Student Engagement
  • Help Teach Language Lessons
  • Telepresence

According to a recent case study performed in Singaporean elementary schools, the interactive robots “provide visualization of complex ideas, helping children to learn and collaborate better.” At ZUBE Robotics, the intention is not for robots to replace the teacher, but rather assist the teacher and enhance the learning process for students. ZUBE Robots can provide personalized approach to students who have autism or ADHD - a population susceptible to falling behind in the classroom.

Business

Meeting Room

  • Facial Recognition and Note-Taking
  • Translate Conversations in Realtime
  • Telepresence for those who may not be able to attend

Day-to-Day Operations

  • Perform Calculations and Trend-Analyses
  • Trek a Warehouse and Manage Inventory
  • Send Emails and Monitor Social Media

The ZUBE Robot has a plethora of applications in the business sphere. The robot can increase productivity and efficiency of day-to-day operations. In the meeting room, the ZUBE Robot can use a combination of its facial recognition, microphone array, and AI to recognize who’s speaking and automatically take notes. Meeting transcripts can be directly saved to the ZUBE Cloud. Furthermore, these robots can detect trends in the global marketplace and stock market and provide suitable recommendations.

The Future

Personal Uses

Personal Assistant

  • Alarm clock, family planner, and vacuum
  • Recipes, traffic, weather updates, and other practical information

Smart Home

  • Watch and secure your home
  • Sends alerts for suspicious activity
  • Temperature sensor and sensing carbon monoxide/smoke

Recreational

  • Videogames

The ZUBE Robot has more personality and mobility than its Google Home and Alexa predecessors. In addition, ZUBE Robot can integrate with other smart home devices (i.e. Pebble Temperature Sensor and Nest Thermostat) through the ZUBE Hub, making the process of securing and maintaining the home easier and more centralized.

Societal Uses

  • Assist first responders (firefighters, police officers, and ambulance)
  • Deliver resources to underserved communities
  • Help the elderly with day-to-day functions

Along with personal uses, the ZUBE robot has the potential to help society as a whole. The ZUBE robot can care for vulnerable populations such as the elderly, who cannot perform many daily activities, and impoverished populations, such as those in the inner city or developing countries, by providing them resources and guidance. Along with helping vulnerable populations, the ZUBE robot can substitute first responders in dire situations in which it is too dangerous for a firefighter or police officer to intervene in.

Potential Negative Effects

Psychological Dependence on Robots and AI

As the ZUBE robot becomes more ever present in our life, it is likely we will become too dependent and attached to the robot. Though it may benefit the human, a dependency may be harmful to the human or institution using it.

Maintenance Costs

The robot may suffer damage and that would require repair of the robot. Whether a component needs to be replaced or a rewiring is mandated, this is a problem for the users of the robot.

Human Job Displacement

Jobs in the industrial stage or in the classroom may be taken over by the ZUBE robot. The robot has higher productivity than a human does. However, this increased productivity may only be useful in an industrial sphere: robots cannot replace service jobs such as teaching, writing, and care-taking.

Cybersecurity Issues

As the ZUBE robot is connected to WiFi and cellular networks, it has the potential to be hacked. Although we here at ZUBE Robotics are doing everything we can to secure these robots from the threats of cybersecurity, there is still potential harm in that a person from across the globe can take control of your personal ZUBE Robot. This may be minor in a personal sphere; however, for instance in the medical sphere, this may be deadly as a ZUBE robot can give a patient a wrong dose.

Cost Structure

According to International Federation of Robotics, the total number of professional service robots sold in 2015 rose considerably by 25% to 41,060 units up from 32,939 in 2014. The sales value increased by 14% to US $4.6 billion. Since 1998, a total of about 220,000 service robots for professional use have been counted in these statistics. In 2015, about 5.4 million service robots for personal and domestic use were sold, 16% more than in 2014. The value of sales increased by 4% to US $2.2 billion.

Regarding the projections for the period till 2019, according to IFR, sales forecast indicate an increase to circa 333,200 units with a value of US $23.1 billion. It is projected that sales of all types of robots for domestic tasks could reach almost 31 million units in the period 2016-2019, with an estimated value of US $13.2 billion.

Our goal at ZUBE Robotics has always been to get a ZUBE robot in every person’s hand. That is how we keep our prices so low: each ZUBE robot cost only $250 and for every 5 ZUBEs sold, a ZUBE robot will be donated to a family in a developing country or inner city community. We can keep our prices low, and at the same time help the community, because we participate in the Fair Trade movement in which we use manufacturers from developing countries to make our robots and we provide them fair prices for their work.
