STEM @ HEBRON

Science Courses at Hebron

AP Chemistry

This 36-week advanced-level course is designed to provide a comprehensive first-year college-level chemistry experience. Emphasis is placed on preparation for the Advanced Placement Chemistry exam. In addition, students partake in labs and other related activities that provide a further understanding.

Medical Terminology

This course is a Latin and Greek language-based class that studies the structure of medical terminology. Employability skills are emphasized. This is a fast-paced class that encourages collaborative, as well as independent learning. Requires good attendance and a motivated learner.

Anatomy and Physiology

A study of the structure and function of human anatomy, including the neuroendocrine, integumentary, and musculoskeletal, digestive, urinary, reproductive, respiratory, and circulatory systems. Content may be either integrated or specialized.

Medical Microbiology

This course offers an in-depth study of viruses, bacteria, fungi, and protists. Emphasis is on the knowledge of relationships between microbes and health maintenance and the role of microbes in infectious disease.

Pathophysiology

This course offers a study in the mechanisms and processes of pathology. Emphasis will be placed on knowing a variety of human diseases and the effects of disease prevention and control.

Technology Courses at Hebron

Computer Programming I (9-12)

Students participate in a coding class in which they learn fundamental coding functions. Codes range from simple to complex and allow for a deeper understanding of the nuances of programing.

Computer Programming II (9-12)

An extension of Computer Programing I. Students approach more complex codes and coding systems. Assignments are comparably more difficult than the first edition of the class.

AP Computer Science (10-12)

Preparatory class for the AP Computer Science exam. Students are given more independent assignments and participate in many labs. Prerequisite knowledge of programming or enrollment in a previous programming class recommended.

Digital Media (9-12)

Students will engage in the use of various forms of media, ranging from many computer programs that are offered. Allow for kids to garner a deeper understanding of the many manipulations of media.

Graphic Design and Illustration I (10-12)

This entry-level course is for training in the visual communication portion of all media businesses. Here you can learn about a career in the many kinds of media and graphic design and illustration jobs. Digital photography and working towards the intermediate level use of image editing and drawing programs are strongly focused upon in this class. This is helpful for any future photographer, videographer, animator or advertiser.

Web Technologies (9-12)

This course provides opportunity to learn about the hardware, software, and connectivity of networks associated with the World Wide Web. Students will create web pages using HTML and graphics software. Students will use digital cameras and scanners to create web page graphics. Students will use the Internet as a research tool.
