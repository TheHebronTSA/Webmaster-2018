STEM @ HEBRON

STEM at Hebron has always been one of the school's strong suits. The Science and Math departments at Hebron are some of the best in the district. The teachers instill a tenacious work ethic into the students through the rigor of the courses. The classes prepare Hebron students for success in the post-secondary level by offering students difficulty that parallels what they may see at the college level. The Engineering and Technology sections of Hebron are more focused toward skill-development and helping kids choose a career path. Not only do the students learn handy skills such as coding or basic typing classes, but they can also learn more advanced mechanisms in relation with architecture or graphic design. STEM at Hebron is really designed to assist students achieve success in future endeavours.

Science Courses at Hebron

AP Chemistry

This 36-week advanced-level course is designed to provide a comprehensive first-year college-level chemistry experience. Emphasis is placed on preparation for the Advanced Placement Chemistry exam. In addition, students partake in labs and other related activities that provide a further understanding.

Medical Terminology

This course is a Latin and Greek language-based class that studies the structure of medical terminology. Employability skills are emphasized. This is a fast-paced class that encourages collaborative, as well as independent learning. Requires good attendance and a motivated learner.

Anatomy and Physiology

A study of the structure and function of human anatomy, including the neuroendocrine, integumentary, and musculoskeletal, digestive, urinary, reproductive, respiratory, and circulatory systems. Content may be either integrated or specialized.

Medical Microbiology

This course offers an in-depth study of viruses, bacteria, fungi, and protists. Emphasis is on the knowledge of relationships between microbes and health maintenance and the role of microbes in infectious disease.

Pathophysiology

This course offers a study in the mechanisms and processes of pathology. Emphasis will be placed on knowing a variety of human diseases and the effects of disease prevention and control.

Technology Courses at Hebron

Computer Programming I (9-12)

Students participate in a coding class in which they learn fundamental coding functions. Codes range from simple to complex and allow for a deeper understanding of the nuances of programing.

Computer Programming II (9-12)

An extension of Computer Programing I. Students approach more complex codes and coding systems. Assignments are comparably more difficult than the first edition of the class.

AP Computer Science (10-12)

Preparatory class for the AP Computer Science exam. Students are given more independent assignments and participate in many labs. Prerequisite knowledge of programming or enrollment in a previous programming class recommended.

Digital Media (9-12)

Students will engage in the use of various forms of media, ranging from many computer programs that are offered. Allow for kids to garner a deeper understanding of the many manipulations of media.

Graphic Design and Illustration I (10-12)

This entry-level course is for training in the visual communication portion of all media businesses. Here you can learn about a career in the many kinds of media and graphic design and illustration jobs. Digital photography and working towards the intermediate level use of image editing and drawing programs are strongly focused upon in this class. This is helpful for any future photographer, videographer, animator or advertiser.

Web Technologies (9-12)

This course provides opportunity to learn about the hardware, software, and connectivity of networks associated with the World Wide Web. Students will create web pages using HTML and graphics software. Students will use digital cameras and scanners to create web page graphics. Students will use the Internet as a research tool.

Engineering Courses at Hebron

Engineering Design and Presentation (9-12)

Involved in many engineering ventures that students can partake. Assignments are given and are shared with the class or, on most occasions, just shared with the teacher. Give students the opportunity to blossom interest in engineering as a career path.

Engineering Design and Problem Solving (Bioscience)

In Engineering Design and Problem Solving for the Biosciences, students explore bioscience engineering using their math, science, and biotechnology backgrounds to solve engineering problems in the life science field. Students engaged in experiments and hands-on activities at least 40% of the time.

Engineering Design and Problem Solving (Digital Electronics)

In Engineering Design and Problem Solving for Digital Electronics, students apply knowledge from previous mathematics, science, and engineering courses to develop problem-solving skills using Digital Electronics. Students engage in skill development and hands-on activities at least 40% of the time. Special emphasis is placed on current trends in technology including robotics and circuit simulation.

Engineering Mathematics

Engineering Mathematics is a course where students solve and model robotic design problems. Students use a variety of mathematical methods and models to represent and analyze problems involving data acquisition, spatial applications, electrical measurement, manufacturing processes, and materials engineering.

Robotics

In Robotics, students build a knowledge base for working with computer-controlled devices. Students use the engineering design process to create prototypes, test, analyze, and improve designs. The focus is on basic robot construction and programming for robotic control. This is a project-based environment that requires students to build prototypes or use simulation software to test designs.

Mathematics Courses at Hebron

Algebra 1

In Algebra I, students will study linear, quadratic, and exponential functions and their related transformations, equations, and associated solutions. In addition, students will study polynomials of degree one and two, radical expressions, sequences, and laws of exponents.

Geometry

In Geometry, students will explore concepts covering coordinate and transformational geometry; logical argument and constructions; proof and congruence; similarity, proof, and trigonometry; two- and three-dimensional figures; circles; and probability.

AP Calculus AB

In this rigorous course, students will examine graphs, functions, derivatives, integrals, continuity, and many other aspects pertaining to single variables. This course is designed to develop mathematical knowledge conceptually and students are guided to connect topics and representations throughout the course to accurately solve diverse types of problems.

AP Statistics

In this course students will broaden their knowledge of variability and statistical processes. Students will also study sampling and experimentation, categorical and quantitative data, probability and random variables, inference, and bivariate data.

Mathematical Models with Applications

This mathematics course provides a path for students to succeed in Algebra II and prepares them for various post-secondary choices. Students learn to apply mathematics through experiences in personal finance, science, engineering, fine arts, and social sciences.

Foundations of College Mathematics

Students will study linear, quadratic, polynomial, rational and radical expressions, equations, and functions as well as probability and statistics. Foundations for College Math is designed to help students meet college entrance requirements (TSI testing) and to be ready for entry-level college math coursework.
